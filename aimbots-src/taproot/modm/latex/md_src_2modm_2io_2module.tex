\hypertarget{md_src_2modm_2io_2module_autotoc_md12}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{Redirecting IOStreams}{Redirecting IOStreams}}\label{md_src_2modm_2io_2module_autotoc_md12}
The {\ttfamily \doxylink{classmodm_1_1_i_o_device_wrapper}{modm::\+\+IODevice\+Wrapper}} transforms any peripheral device that provides static {\ttfamily write()} and {\ttfamily read()} functions into an {\ttfamily IODevice}.

You have to decide what happens when the device buffer is full and you cannot write to it at the moment. There are two options:\+


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item busy wait until the buffer is free, or
\item discard the bytes that cannot be written.
\end{DoxyEnumerate}

Option 1 has the advantage, that none of your data will be lost, however, busy-\/waiting can take a long time and can mess up your program timings. There is also a {\bfseries{high risk of deadlock}}, when writing to a IODevice inside of an interrupt and then busy-\/waiting forever because the IODevice requires interrupts itself to send out the data.

It is therefore highly recommended to use option 2, where surplus data will be discarded. You should increase the IODevice buffer size, if you experience missing data from your connection. This behavior is also deadlock safe when called from inside another interrupt, and your program timing is minimally affected (essentially only coping data into the buffer).

There is no default template argument, so that you hopefully make a conscious decision and be aware of this behavior.
